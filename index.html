HazardNet

AI-powered disaster prediction and emergency response platform for safer communities in Sri Lanka.

Abstract

HazardNet is an AI-based natural disaster prediction and SOS emergency response system tailored for Sri Lanka. The system leverages Machine Learning, Computer Vision, and Natural Language Processing to provide real-time, personalized risk assessments for floods and landslides, manage rescue operations, verify disaster reports, and coordinate donations effectively during critical situations.

Explore Domain

Domain

Information gathered through literature survey, problem definition, and methodologies.

Literature Survey

Extensive research was conducted on existing disaster management platforms worldwide and local systems used in Sri Lanka. The survey revealed that while broad, regional alerts exist, there is a distinct lack of predictive, personalized systems that evaluate dynamic environmental factors to issue localized safety risk percentages in real-time.

Research Gap

There is a critical lack of probability-based, location-specific risk scoring for individuals. Current platforms fail to integrate real-time user location, elevation data, dynamic river distance, and machine learning to offer individualized safe routing and home safety verification prior to disaster impact.

Research Problem

Sri Lanka is frequently affected by floods and landslides, especially during heavy rainfall. Existing systems provide general, regional alerts. Users need personalized and location-specific safety information. A user needs to know whether their current location, home, or route is safe. Disaster risk is not a simple yes/no; it requires probability-based scoring.

Research Objectives

  • Develop a machine learning-based flood and landslide risk prediction component.
  • Integrate real-time weather and location APIs to evaluate immediate area safety.
  • Support safe route planning to help users avoid dangerous areas.
  • Provide an SOS emergency reporting mechanism with nearby service locations.
  • Manage rescue camp capacity using computer vision and facilitate targeted donations.
